About Me

Welcome to my personal website. I am a 5th year PhD candidate in Mechanical Engineering at McGill University in Montreal, working at the intersection of artificial intelligence, additive manufacturing, and composites. My research focuses on developing AI-based sensing and decision-making tools to support process monitoring, defect detection, and quality evaluation in advanced manufacturing systems. I am supervised by Dr. Yaoyao Fiona Zhao at Additive Design and Manufacturing Laboratory (ADML).

My PhD research is also supervised by Dr. Guy Lamouche at Digital Technologies Research Center (DT) of the National Research Council Canada (NRC). AT NRC, I have worked with Dr. Priti Wanjara, Dr. Javad Gholipour, Dr. Christophe Bescond, and Dr. Sheida Sarafan as part of the 3+2 Canada-Germany project Artificial Intelligence Enhancement of Process Sensing for Adaptive Laser Additive Manufacturing (AI-SLAM). In AI-SLAM, I have led the research, development and deployment of AI solutions on production machines with close collaboration of industry and research partners (Apollo-Clad Laser Cladding, Fraunhofer Institute for Laser Techology ILT, NRC, Braintoy AI, BCT GmbH).

In addition to AI-SLAM, I have been part of research collaborations with the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST, Dr. Yan Lu), Arizona State University (ASU, Dr. Hyunwoong Ko), Nanyang Technological University (NTU, Dr. Seung Ki Moon), and Agency for Science, Technology and Research (A*STAR, Dr. Lequn Chen). These collaborations have broadly explored the applications of transfer learning across manufacturing technologies and sensing modalities to expedite the adoption of data-driven additive manufacutring research.

My research at the Materials Data Science and Informatics (IAS-9) group involved investigation of vision transformer models for segmenting additively manufactured microstructures.

Prior to attending McGill, I was a full-time researcher at the Korea STEP Center in Daejeon. At K-STEP, I developed a PLM-MES interface to link design information with manufacturing instructions and feedback for highly customized designs with a case study of ship design data from Daewoo Shipbuilding and Marine Engineering (DSME).

I completed my MS in Mechanical Engineering at the Korea Advanced Institute of Science and Technology (KAIST) in Daejeon. My master's research focused on geometric and topological analysis for inter-CAD model translation. My MS thesis on persistent identification for parametric CAD data exchange was advised by Dr. Soonhung Han at the Intelligent Computer-Aided Design (iCAD) group. At iCAD, I was also mentored by Dr. Soonjo Kwon.

I completed my BS in Mechanical Engineering with honors from the University of Engineering and Technology (UET) in Lahore.
